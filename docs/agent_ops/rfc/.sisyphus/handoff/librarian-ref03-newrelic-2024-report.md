# New Relic 2024 Observability Forecast — Summary for RFC Context

**Source:** New Relic 2024 Observability Forecast (Oct 2024)
**Methodology:** 1,700 technology professionals surveyed across 16 countries (65% practitioners, 35% ITDMs). Conducted Apr–May 2024 by ETR.

---

## Tool Fragmentation

- **45% of organizations use 5+ tools** for observability
- Only **25% have achieved full-stack observability**
- **2-to-1 preference** for a single consolidated platform over multiple point solutions
- Single-tool adoption **increased 37% YoY**; average number of tools **decreased 11% YoY**
- **41% plan to consolidate tools** within the next year
- Organizations shifting from fragmented monitoring toward consolidated observability platforms

## Cost of Outages & Downtime

- **High-impact outages cost up to $1.9M/hour**; 62% said ≥$1M/hour
- **Median annual downtime from high-impact outages: 77 hours**
- Engineering teams spend **30% of their time** (12 hrs/week) managing outages and disruptions
- **Median annual observability spend: $1.95M**; median annual value received: **$8.15M** (4x ROI / 295%)
- Full-stack observability orgs experienced **79% less downtime** (70 hrs vs 338 hrs/year), saving **$42M**
- Full-stack orgs had **48% lower hourly outage costs** ($1.1M vs $2.1M) and **27% lower annual observability spend**
- Business observability users saw **24% lower hourly outage costs** and **25% less time** addressing disruptions

## Investigation & Resolution Workflows

- Top practices reducing downtime: **root cause analysis & post-incident reviews** (37%), **DORA metrics** (34%), **golden signals tracking** (33%), **MTTx management** (33%)
- Full-stack observability orgs were **51% more likely to detect and resolve issues early**
- Robust observability practices **reduced time spent on disruptions by 44%**
- Leading outage causes: network failure (35%), third-party/cloud provider failure (29%), human error (28%)

## AI & Agent Trends

- **41% cited AI as the primary driver** for adopting observability (tied with security as #1 driver)
- **42% deploying AI monitoring**, 29% ML model monitoring, 24% AIOps
- **39% plan to deploy AIOps** within the next year; 36% plan AI monitoring; 34% ML model monitoring
- Organizations with AI-driven observability reported **higher annual value and stronger ROI**
- Security monitoring was the most deployed capability at 58%

## Open Source

- **51% use open-source** for at least one observability capability, but **only 1% rely solely on OSS**
- Common OSS uses: AI monitoring (31%), synthetic monitoring (28%), distributed tracing (28%), AIOps (26%)

---

## Key Takeaways for RFC Problem Statement

1. **Fragmentation is the norm, not the exception** — nearly half of orgs juggle 5+ tools, directly increasing complexity and cost
2. **The cost of context-switching is enormous** — 30% of engineering time lost to disruption management, much of it navigating between tools
3. **Consolidation momentum is strong** — 2:1 preference for unified platforms, 37% YoY growth in single-tool adoption
4. **AI is accelerating complexity** — AI monitoring is now the #2 deployed capability, creating new observability demands
5. **Investigation workflows are the bottleneck** — RCA, DORA metrics, and MTTx management are top practices, but require correlated data across the stack
6. **Full-stack visibility delivers outsized returns** — 79% less downtime, 48% lower costs, 51% faster detection
